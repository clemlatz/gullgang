# Rules

## Introduction

Gull Gang is a card game for 2 to 8 players, heavily inspired by the classic "Tamalou" but much better
because there are seagulls.

The game is offered as "print and play" under a Creative Commons license, meaning you can download the
cards and print them yourself.

## Objective

In **Gull Gang**, the goal is to have the *lowest score* possible.

At the end of a round, each player adds up the values of the cards laid in front of them to get their score. This
score is then added to the scores from previous rounds to get the player's total score.

The game ends when one player's total score reaches or exceeds 100 points.
The player with the lowest total score at that point wins.

Gull Gang is a memory game. Once a player has placed cards face down in front of them, they may no longer
look at them (unless the power of a drawn card allows it). Players must therefore memorize the values of the
cards on the table.

## Card Anatomy

Each card has a value, shown in a coloured circle in the top-left corner. This value is added to the player's score
at the end of each round.

Some cards also have a power, which *may* be used if the card is discarded immediately after being drawn. Using a
card's power is never mandatory.

## Round Structure

For the first round, the oldest player deals. The next round, it will be their left neighbour, and so on.

### Setup

1. Deal four cards to each player
2. Each player lays their cards face down in a square in front of them
3. Each player looks at their two nearest cards, then places them back face down
4. Form a draw pile with the remaining cards

The goal at the end of a round is to have the lowest score possible, calculated for each player by adding up the
values of the cards in front of them.

During each round, players will try to get rid of high-value cards by discarding them or swapping them with
other players for lower-value ones.

### Turn Order

Play goes clockwise. The player sitting after the dealer goes first.

1. The active player draws a card without showing it. They may choose to:
    - discard one of their cards and replace it with the drawn card;
    - use the power of the drawn card (if it has one), then discard it;
    - discard the drawn card (without using its power, if it has one).
2. The next player may then choose to:
    - discard one of their cards and replace it with the card just discarded by the previous player;
    - draw a card, then take one of the three actions described in step 1.
3. And so on…

### End of a Round

After finishing their turn, a player may call "Gull Gang!" if they think they have the lowest score at
the table. One final turn is then played, ending with the player who made the call.

Each player reveals their cards and adds up their values to get their score for that round.

If the player who called "Gull Gang!" has the lowest score of the round, they score 0 points.
Otherwise, they score their normal score plus a 10-point penalty.

Round scores are added to previous round scores to produce the total score.

## Special Moves

### Low Swoop

At any time, when the active player discards a card during their turn, another player may immediately discard a
card of the same value from their own hand.

Play then continues normally, as if the quick discard had not happened.

If multiple players hold a matching card and attempt a quick discard at the same time, the first to place their
card on the discard pile wins; the others take their card back.

If a player makes a mistake and discards a card that does not match the last discarded card, they put the card
back and draw an extra card to place in front of them.

### Dive Bomb — 3 or more players

It is also possible to use the quick discard to discard a card belonging to an opponent. The same rules as the
quick discard apply.

If a player discards a card that belonged to another player, they must give one of their own cards to that player,
who places it in front of them.
